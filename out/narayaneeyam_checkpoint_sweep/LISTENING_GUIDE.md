# Blind checkpoint comparison

The `blind` directory contains six candidates (`A` through `F`). Each candidate
contains the same four validation verses. Listen by verse first—for example,
play `eval_nar_010_002_1.wav` from A through F—rather than listening to all of
one candidate at once.

For every candidate, score each category from 1 (poor) to 5 (excellent):

1. Speaker identity: does it sound like the target speaker?
2. Sanskrit accuracy: vowels, aspiration, conjuncts, anusvara/visarga, and no
   omitted or repeated syllables.
3. Metre and pacing: stable cadence, natural pauses, and no rushed ending.
4. Audio quality: clarity, stability, and absence of buzz, metallic sound,
   warble, clicks, or breath-like hallucinations.

Reject a candidate with a serious textual error even when its voice similarity
is strong. Record a preferred candidate plus a runner-up. Do not inspect
`checkpoint_key.json` until the listening decision is recorded.
